DC Inductor

In variable frequency drives (VFDs), inductors can be placed after the drive’s input diodes, between the input rectifier and the DC bus link. This configuration, known as a DC bus choke, DC link choke, or DC reactor, is typically used to smooth out the DC output and improve system efficiency.

DC bus chokes are generally integrated into the drive and can be installed in pairs or as a single device on either the positive or negative bus. These chokes help resist sudden changes in current, reducing ripple currents in the rectified DC output, and protect the DC load or motor from high starting current, harmonics, and ripple content present in the DC supply line.

Features of Electric DC Choke

  • High Efficiency
  • Easy to Operate
  • Long Service Life
  • Protects from Damage
  • Suitable for Reducing the Rectified DC Output

Benefits of DC Chokes

  • Used with drives and on the output side of controllers and rectifiers
  • Acts as a power supply filter, accurately meeting the device's needs
  • Reduces the effect of ripple current in the rectified DC output
  • Valid for reducing harmonics at the input terminal of the device
  • Low maintenance and available at affordable prices
